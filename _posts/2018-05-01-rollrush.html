“When nature calls, there's not much you can do. You just have to deal with it. Fortunately, there's an invaluable friend to help you out. A simple toilet roll can be the line between success and disaster. But have you ever wondered how your toilet roll might feel when it gets used?”

Roll Rush is the second game I developed for Android. It's a casual endless runner where you control a toilet roll that must navigate through various obstacles while collecting toilet paper sheets to keep going. The longer you survive, the harder it gets as the speed increases and more obstacles appear.

The game features simple tap controls to make the toilet roll switch between 5 lanes, jump to dodge obstacles and collect powerups. The road, along with all obstacles and collectibles, is procedurally generated, making each run unique.

The obstacles include puddles of water and cars, which cause game over, and poops, which halve the roll's size when hit. Collecting toilet paper sheets replenishes the roll's size, allowing it to keep rolling. There are also power-ups such as shield, time slower, roll switch, and rocket that provide temporary advantages to help you survive longer. You can also collect coins to buy extra lives to use when a run ends.

Beyond normal power-ups, there are special money chests that randomly spawn during a run. Opening these chests lets you earn extra coins for a limited time. The rarest power-up is the teleportation device, which launches the roll far ahead on the road, helping you reach greater distances.

When developing Roll Rush, I fully integrated the Play Games Services SDK to provide features like leaderboards and achievements, enhancing the competitive aspect of the game.
