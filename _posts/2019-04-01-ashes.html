“It's hot down here...”

This is my first completed halo CE map. This project was started years ago, even before the release of the MCC on PC with its new and improved Mod Tools. It was an attempt to see how far I could push myself with design, modeling, texturing. The map has gone through many changes as it was being worked on. Some changes were due to artistic choice and some changes were necessary to mitigate the engine limitations I was discovering along the way. After a couple years of abandonment, it saw new life thanks to the release of the Mod Tools; I decided to resurrect it and finally finish it once and for all.

The map uses custom geometry, custom textures and shaders, custom sounds and custom particle effects. However, it does not feature any new mechanics, weapons or characters, as I wanted to keep the classic Halo feel as much as possible.

I'm a fan of flowing liquids in games. I find it very fascinating how developers represent things like rivers and flowing things in games, and I feel it's an art in itself to be able to represent them realistically. I wanted to incorporate this element in my map and I landed on lava. This is mainly because I wanted to do something different; volcanic environments are not featured at all in Halo games, while water is quite common. The inspiration for the bases comes from the later Halo titles rather than the original trilogy. This is especially evident in the shapes of the geometry and light pattern strips.

The old map

The map went through several iterations before reaching its final form. Here are some screenshots of the older version of the map, back when it was still a work in progress.

Early base design, later scrapped due to scale issues that made it feel too small compared to the player model.

The ground geometry was left unchanged.

Another scrapped idea was the addition of “lava pipes”. Since they never really had a clear purpose, I decided to cut them in the end.

More of the scrapped lava pipes. It's also possible to see the previous position of the crashed Pelican.

The skybox originally included a smoke and ash column, inspired by the Halo 4 map “Shatter”. This element was later scrapped.

The original skybox included a full terrain with massive lava flows painted in Substance Painter using the Physical Paintbrush. I eventually scrapped it due to rendering issues with fog.

Lore

Installation 01 is a distant, forgotten and dangerous place. Its discovery by scientist Robin Hale sparked an insatiable interest to explore the surface. Radio scans turned out to be inconclusive, as the thick atmosphere that surrounds the seemingly deserted ring has proven almost impenetrable. A group of Spartans was sent to investigate. However, they realized a little too late that the ashy, corrosive air was too destructive for the ships they used to reach the surface.
